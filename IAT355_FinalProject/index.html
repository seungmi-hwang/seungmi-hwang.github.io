Growth of Generative AI Tools

Exploring the growth and regional popularity of ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude using Google Trends data from 2021 to 2026.

Explore Project

Project Overview

Our project explores the growth and regional popularity of major generative AI tools, focusing on ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude. Using Google Trends data, we examine how public interest in these platforms has changed over time and how it differs across selected countries. This initial exploration helps us better understand the available data, identify its limitations, and refine the scope of our final project.

This line chart illustrates the growth of generative AI tools over time using Google Trends data from 2021 to 2026. ChatGPT rises sharply starting in late 2022 and remains the dominant platform throughout the selected period. In the next stage, users will be able to filter individual platforms and explore specific time periods to better understand how interest changed over time.

Figure 2. Regional Comparison of Generative AI Interest

Data source: Google Trends (2021–2026, by regions)

This chart compares search interest in ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude across the United States, the United Kingdom, India, and Canada. ChatGPT shows the highest level of interest in all selected countries, confirming its dominant position in the generative AI market. Gemini shows relatively stronger interest in India, suggesting faster growth in an emerging market, while Claude remains lower overall due to its later adoption.

These four countries were selected to represent different levels of technological development and user adoption. The United States serves as a major technology hub, while the United Kingdom and Canada represent developed markets with relatively stable adoption patterns. India was included as a high-growth market with increasing public interest in AI technologies.

Future interaction will allow users to compare selected countries and focus on individual AI tools for a more detailed regional analysis.

Data Challenges

During our data exploration, several challenges were identified that affect how the results should be interpreted:

  • Normalized Data: Google Trends provides relative search interest rather than actual user counts.
  • Uneven Platform History: Claude has less historical data due to its later release.
  • Category Inconsistency: Platforms may be categorized differently, which can affect comparability.
  • Regional Limitation: Regional analysis is mainly available at the country level.
  • Missing Purpose-based Data: No reliable public dataset was available for comparing usage purposes such as writing, coding, or idea generation.

Refined Scope

Based on the initial data exploration, the project scope has been refined to ensure clarity and feasibility. The focus of the project is now on analyzing time-based growth trends of generative AI tools and comparing platform popularity across different regions.

We originally considered including a purpose-based comparison, such as how people use these tools for writing, coding, or brainstorming. However, because reliable and comparable public data was not available, this part was removed from the current scope.

Explore the Data (Planned Interactive Features)

Based on our initial exploration, we plan to add interactive elements that allow users to engage more actively with the data.

  • Filter by platform to compare ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude
  • Filter by time period to focus on specific years or adoption phases
  • Hover interactions to reveal exact values and highlight major moments of growth

These interactions will enrich the storytelling of the project by allowing users to explore the trends in a more detailed and personalized way.
